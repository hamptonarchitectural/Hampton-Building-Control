What Are Building Regulations?

Almost every project requires Building Regulations, this can be in the form of a simple 'Building Notice' for minor works to 'Full Plans' application. Everyone has a part to play to ensure your project is safe and meets current regulations.

  • What Does It Mean For You

    You're required by law to ensure your project complies with Building Regulations, this means you must engage a Principal Designer and Principal Contractor.

  • What's Our Role

    Our responsibilities extend beyond drawings and submissions. We're legally required to act as your Principal Designer and must ensure your project is designed safely.

  • And What About The Builder

    Your builder assumes the role of the Principal Contractor, meaning they're legally required to ensure the project is built safely.

Do I Need Building Control?

Some projects are exempt, while others require a formal application. Here’s a guide to help you understand your responsibilities.

Minor Alterations

Repairs, replacements, maintenance, small detached buildings (under 15m²), most porches, conservatories, and carports.

Building Control Not Required

Competent Persons Scheme

Work by registered installers for gas (GasSafe), electrics (NICEIC, ELECSA), windows (FENSA, CERTAS), or solid fuel appliances.

Not Required (If certified)

Extensions

Any extension to your property, including single storey, double storey, side, or rear additions.

Building Control Required

Conversions

Converting any part of your property for a new purpose, such as a loft or garage conversion.

Building Control Required

New Dwellings

The construction of any new home, including a house, bungalow, or other type of self-contained dwelling.

Building Control Required

Important Note:

Building Control approval is separate from Planning Permission. It's often required even for projects that fall under Permitted Development or Prior Approval rights.
